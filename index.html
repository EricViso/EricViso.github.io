What is Cryogeny?

Cryogeny, or cryopreservation, is the science of preserving biological material at very low temperatures to maintain its structural integrity and functionality. The primary goal is to prevent cellular damage during the freezing process, which typically occurs at temperatures below -150Â°C.

Microscopic visualization of successfully cryopreserved tissue

Key aspects of cryopreservation:

  • Use of cryoprotectants to prevent ice crystal formation
  • Controlled cooling rates to minimize cellular stress
  • Vitrification techniques to achieve glass-like state
